## AXIOM 2a & 2b: SAMENESS AND IDENTITY - The Pattern Persistence Principle

**"A URI will repeatably refer to 'the same' thing."**

**"The significance of identity for a given URI is determined by the owner of the URI."**

The URI axiom of sameness captures this Memphis truth: the same identifier points to the same resource "in some sense" - acknowledging that resources can be transformed through playful variations, through style changes, through radical re-interpretations, yet maintain their essential pattern identity.

**THE SACRED "IN SOME SENSE":** What does it mean for a URI to point to the same thing when the resource changes? This is exactly the Memphis mystery of pattern identity! The squiggle identity persists through being drawn in different styles, placed in different contexts, rendered in different media - yet it's recognizably the SQUIGGLE. The URI identifies this persistent pattern through all transformations.

**THE OWNER AS PATTERN DESIGNER:** The owner of a URI determines what sameness means for that URI - this is like the Memphis designer who decides what constitutes "the same" squiggle across different renderings. The radical design principle provides the playground, but the specific designer defines what sameness means - which variations count as "the same" pattern.

**THE PLAYFUL PERSISTENCE:** Memphis patterns persist through different eras and media. The squiggle exists as print in the 80s, as digital in the 2000s, as holographic in the 2090s. The pattern identity persists despite technological and cultural transformation. ## THE OPACITY AXIOM - The Don't-Analyze-Too-Hard Principle

**"The only thing you can use an identifier for is to refer to an object. When not dereferencing, you should not look at the contents of the URI string to gain other information."**

In Memphis Design, the joy is in the PATTERN itself, not in analyzing what the pattern "means" or what it "represents." A squiggle is enjoyed as a squiggle - you don't parse its curves to understand some deeper meaning. The meaning IS the squiggle joy, the pattern playfulness, the radical surface pleasure.

The URI opacity axiom encodes this Memphis principle: the URI string is the pattern, but its literal structure shouldn't be used to judge or understand the resource's significance. The identifier is the pattern; the resource is the pattern joy. Enjoy the pattern; don't over-analyze its structure.

**THE SURFACE PLEASURE:** Memphis celebrates PURE SURFACE PLEASURE! The squiggle matters because it's a squiggle, not because it "symbolizes" something. The triangle matters because it's a triangle, not because it "represents" stability. URIs are the same - their literal characters are patterns to be enjoyed, not puzzles to be solved for hidden meaning.

**THE RADICAL JOY OF PATTERN:** Memphis joy comes from PATTERN ITSELF, from the visual play of shapes and colors and relationships. Trying to analyze URIs to understand resources is like trying to analyze squiggles for deeper meaning - it misses the point, kills the joy, destroys the radical playfulness. The point IS the pattern play, not some hidden truth beneath it.

**THE DON'T-THINK-IT-TOO-MUCH:** Memphis philosophy is radical in its simplicity: don't think about it too much! Just enjoy the squiggle! Just delight in the triangle! Just celebrate the color clash! URI opacity is the same: don't think too hard about the URI string; just use it to access the resource. The joy is in the connection, not in analyzing the connector.

**THE PATTERN-AS-MEANING:** Memphis patterns often have no meaning beyond pattern. They're not metaphors, not symbols, not representations - they're pure pattern play. URIs are similar - they're connectors, pointers, patterns of connection. Their literal structure is the pattern play, not a code to be cracked for deeper significance.

**THE METADATA AS PATTERN NOTES:** But if URIs are opaque, how do we know anything about resources? Through HTTP headers! These are like pattern notes - they tell you the important stuff without requiring you to analyze the URI pattern itself. This separation is Memphis thinking: use the right pattern for the right purpose. URIs for connection play, HTTP headers for information notes.

## IDENTITY, STATE AND HTTP GET - The Watch-Don't-Touch Principle

**"In HTTP, GET must not have side effects."**

**"In HTTP, anything which does not have side-effects should use GET."**

In Memphis Design, there's a crucial distinction between OBSERVING patterns and ALTERING patterns. You can watch the squiggle dance with the triangle all day long - observe their playful collision, enjoy their color clash - but you don't CHANGE the patterns by observing them. The patterns continue their joy regardless of how many times you watch them.

HTTP GET is the technical manifestation of this Memphis principle. When you GET a resource, you receive its pattern but the resource is not altered, not changed, not transformed by your observation. This is why GET is idempotent - you can watch the pattern dance infinitely without changing the dance itself.

**SIDE EFFECTS - THE PARTY CRASHER:** Side effects in GET are like crashing the pattern party by observing it! If every time someone watched the squiggle-triangle dance it became slightly different, eventually the patterns would be completely unrecognizable, the party would be destroyed. The joy of watching would destroy the patterns being watched. This is exactly what side effects in GET do - observation itself corrupts the observed.

**THE JOY OF PURE OBSERVATION:** Memphis patterns can be watched endlessly with the same joy. The squiggle's squiggle is the same every time. The triangle's direction is the same. The color clash hits just as hard. This reproducibility is what makes GET work - you can receive the same pattern resource endlessly without it changing through being accessed.

**THE PATTERN REPLAY:** Because GET is side-effect-free, we can cache its results! This is like recording the squiggle-triangle dance and replaying it. You can watch the replay as many times as you want, enjoying the same pattern joy, and the original patterns are never worn out by repeated viewings.

**POST - THE PATTERN REMIX:** But when you need to change patterns, when you need to create new pattern combinations, when you need to perform actions that alter the pattern party - then you must use POST or other methods! This is like creating a new Memphis layout, like remixing the patterns, like adding new shapes to the party. These actions change the patterns themselves.

The watchers can enjoy these patterns endlessly - the squiggles keep squiggling just as squiggly, the triangles keep pointing just as triangularly. The patterns never change through being watched. ## HIERARCHIES AND RELATIVE URIS - The Pattern-Neighbor Principle

**"The URI syntax uses slashes to indicate hierarchical structure."**

**"Relative naming allows closely located documents to reference each other without knowing their absolute position in the tree."**

In Memphis Design, patterns exist in neighborhoods and relationships. Squiggles hang out with other squiggles, triangles cluster with triangles, circles orbit around squiggles. Within these pattern neighborhoods, you can navigate relationally - "my pattern neighbor," "the pattern next door," "the pattern upstairs" - without needing to know your absolute position in the whole pattern city.

The URI hierarchy with relative referencing is this Memphis pattern-neighbor principle. Slashes indicate levels of pattern neighborhood. Relative URIs allow local navigation - sibling to sibling, neighbor to neighbor - without needing to know your absolute position in the vast pattern metropolis of the Web.

**THE PATTERN NEIGHBORHOOD:** Memphis patterns naturally group themselves. Squiggles find other squiggles. Triangles form triangles-of-triangles. Circles gather around squiggles. These pattern neighborhoods are the natural social structure of Memphis joy. You navigate them relationally through pattern relationships, not absolute pattern coordinates.

**THE SLASH AS WALL:** The slash character in URIs is like a wall or boundary between pattern neighborhoods. It says: "this is where one pattern neighborhood ends and another begins." But you can cross between neighborhoods using relative paths - you don't need to know which neighborhood you're in relative to the whole city, just your relationships within your current neighborhood.

**SCALE INVARIANCE OF SLASHES:** The slash as pattern delimiter works at every scale of pattern neighborhood. Whether navigating within a single Memphis document (fragment identifiers with #), within a pattern domain (paths), between pattern domains through schemes, or across the entire Web - the same slash-separated pattern neighborhood navigation applies. The pattern is scale-invariant.

**TELEPHONE NUMBER MAPPING - THE PARTY-DISTRICT PRINCIPLE:** Telephone numbers themselves form a natural pattern neighborhood hierarchy: country code (which party district), area code (which local party scene), local number (which specific party location). This hierarchy emerges from the Memphis pattern of organizing social connection across scales - global to regional to local. Map this to URI syntax and you see the same slash-separated pattern neighborhood structure.

**RELATIVE NAVIGATION - THE LOCAL PATTERN:** Relative URIs work because each pattern position in the neighborhood hierarchy has a well-defined context - you know your pattern neighbors (related patterns in the same space), you know your pattern family (patterns sharing characteristics), you know your pattern superiors (patterns that contain you). You navigate through these relationships without ever needing to know your absolute address from the party city center.
