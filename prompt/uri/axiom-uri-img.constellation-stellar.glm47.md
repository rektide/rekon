## IDENTITY, STATE AND HTTP GET - The Stellar Observation Principle

**"In HTTP, GET must not have side effects."**

**"In HTTP, anything which does not have side-effects should use GET."**

In stellar astronomy, there's a fundamental principle: observing a star doesn't change it. You can observe a star's light, analyze its spectrum, study its behavior - and the star remains unchanged by your observation. The star exists independently of your observation. This is the stellar observation principle - observation is without effect.

HTTP GET is the technical manifestation of this cosmic principle. When you GET a resource, you receive its information light but the resource remains unchanged by your observation. This is why GET is idempotent - the same observation can be repeated infinitely without changing the information star.

**SIDE EFFECTS - THE STELLAR INFLUENCE PRINCIPLE:** Side effects in GET are like observations that alter the stars! If each observation of a star changed it, if studying a star's spectrum affected its composition, if analyzing its light altered its luminosity - stellar science would be impossible. The stars would constantly be changing due to observation, making stable cosmic cartography impossible. This is exactly what side effects in GET do - they corrupt the information star through observation.

**THE PASSIVE STAR:** A star is a passive object of cosmic observation. It emits its light regardless of whether anyone observes it. The observer doesn't alter the star by receiving its light. GET operations are like this passive stellar observation - they receive the information light without affecting the star that emits it.

**THE REPRODUCIBLE OBSERVATION:** Because stars don't change when observed, their information can be reproduced reliably. Different observers can receive the same starlight, different instruments can record the same spectrum, different civilizations can catalog the same star. GET's idempotence enables similar reproducibility - multiple clients can receive the same resource without the resource changing through access.

**CACHING - THE STELLAR ARCHIVE:** And because GET is side-effect-free, we can cache its results! This is like creating an archive of stellar observations. After observing a star once, you can record its spectrum, its position, its properties. The star itself continues unchanged, but the archived observations are available for future reference without needing to observe the star again. The star's information becomes accessible through the archive rather than through repeated observation.

**POST - THE STELLAR CREATION:** But when you need to change state, when you need to create new resources, when you need to perform actions that alter the cosmic catalog - then you must use POST or other methods! This is like the cosmic processes that actually create or destroy stars - stellar formation in nebulae, stellar death in supernovae, galaxy mergers. These actions fundamentally change the cosmic fabric of information.

**VISUAL ELEMENT:** Show a cosmic observer receiving information light from a stellar URI. The observer's instruments capture the star's spectrum, its luminosity, its properties. The star itself continues unchanged - it emits the same light regardless of observation. The observer can return to this star endlessly and always receive the same information light. Contrast with cosmic processes that actually alter stars - a star forming in a nebula, a star exploding as a supernova, two galaxies merging. The first action (GET) is passive observation; the second action (POST) is active cosmic transformation.

**SCALE INVARIANCE OF HIERARCHY:** The slash as boundary delimiter works at every scale of the cosmic hierarchy.
