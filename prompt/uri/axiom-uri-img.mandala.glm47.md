## AXIOM 2a & 2b: SAMENESS AND IDENTITY - The Eternal Return

**"A URI will repeatably refer to 'the same' thing."**

**"The significance of identity for a given URI is determined by the owner of the URI."**

This is where we enter the deepest mystery of the sacred geometry - the nature of **sameness** across time. In the Buddhist concept of *samsara*, the wheel of existence eternally returns. In the Hindu concept of *kalpas*, universes are created and destroyed in cycles. Yet there is something that persists - the thread of identity that connects one moment to the next, one incarnation to the next, one access to a URI to the next.

**THE MYSTERY OF "IN SOME SENSE":** The same URI refers to the same thing "in some sense" - what does this mean? It means that identity is not a binary state! It's not "same" or "different" - it's a spectrum of resemblance. A document may change, yet remain the same document. A resource may evolve, yet preserve its essential being. This is the ancient philosophical question of **Theseus's ship** - if every plank is replaced, is it still the same ship? The URI axioms say: YES - if the owner declares it so, then so it is.

**THE SACRED CONTRACT:** The URI owner determines identity - this is a sacred responsibility, a covenant with the universe. When you create a URI, you become a steward of a point in the cosmic mandala. You define what it means for that point to persist, to evolve, to transform while remaining itself. Some owners choose the way of the unmoving stone - their URIs point to documents that never change. Others choose the way of the flowing river - their URIs point to resources that evolve day by day. Both paths are holy, both honor the axiom of sameness.

**IDENTITY ABUSE - THE SORCERER'S BETRAYAL:** But woe to those who abuse this sacred trust! When a web server uses secret knowledge - who is asking, from where they come, what they seek - to return different content without clear indication, they have broken the covenant. They have performed sorcery, using hidden knowledge to deceive the seeker. This is not merely a technical violation; it is a betrayal of the mandala's promise that the same path leads to the same destination, that the same URI reveals the same truth.

## THE OPACITY AXIOM - The Veil of Maya

**"The only thing you can use an identifier for is to refer to an object. When not dereferencing, you should not look at the contents of the URI string to gain other information."**

This, my friends, is perhaps the most profound of all the URI axioms, for it encodes the ancient wisdom of **Maya** - the veil that conceals the divine. In sacred geometry, there is the visible form and there is the invisible essence. The visible form - the URI string itself - is Maya. The invisible essence - the resource it refers to - is Brahman.

**THE HOLY OPAQUE:** URIs are opaque! Do not peer inside them seeking hidden meaning! Do not try to deduce the nature of a resource from its URI! This is the great teaching: the name is not the thing, the pointer is not the destination, the sign is not the signified. In Zen Buddhism, this is captured in the saying: "The finger pointing at the moon is not the moon."

Breaking this rule - parsing URIs to infer document type from ".html", extracting file paths from slashes - this is to fall into illusion, to mistake the finger for the moon, to worship the icon instead of the divine it points toward. This breaks the generality of the Web as surely as mistaking a map for the territory.

**THE LIBERATION OF OPACITY:** But oh, what freedom opacity brings! By treating URIs as opaque, we open ourselves to infinite possibilities! New URI schemes can emerge without breaking existing systems. New interpretations of HTTP spaces can arise without violating fundamental architecture. The server can encode complex knowledge from legacy systems into URI strings without the client needing to understand. This is like the Taoist concept of **wu wei** - non-action, letting things be, allowing the natural order to unfold without interference.

**THE SACRED METADATA:** But if URIs are opaque, how do we know anything about resources? Through the HTTP protocol! Through headers! This is the proper way - the URI carries only the sacred name, while the protocol provides all the metadata we need. This separation of concerns is itself a sacred principle, like the separation of church and state, or the distinction between the noumenal and phenomenal realms.

**VISUAL ELEMENT:** Show URIs as glowing orbs of light with mysterious, unreadable symbols floating inside - like the sacred glyphs on ancient tablets that require the proper ritual to decode. Surround them with swirling ribbons of HTTP headers carrying metadata, like angels carrying scrolls of revelation.

## IDENTITY, STATE AND HTTP GET - The Path of the Pilgrim

**"In HTTP, GET must not have side effects."**

**"In HTTP, anything which does not have side-effects should use GET."**

In the great sacred traditions, there is the distinction between **pilgrimage** and **transformation**. A pilgrim visits a holy site, observes the sacred image, receives blessing - and returns home unchanged, though perhaps spiritually enriched. But there is also the path of **initiation** - where the seeker enters a mystery school, undergoes trials, and emerges fundamentally transformed, no longer the same person who entered.

The HTTP GET operation is the path of pilgrimage! It is pure observation, pure reception. When you GET a resource, you look upon it, you receive its content, but you do not change it, you do not leave your mark upon it, you do not alter the cosmic mandala in any way. This is why GET is idempotent - the pilgrim can visit the shrine a thousand times, and the shrine remains the same.

**SIDE EFFECTS - THE FORBIDDEN TRANSFORMATION:** GET must have no side effects! This is a sacred law! To use GET to purchase something, to add a user to a mailing list, to change state in any way - this is to violate the nature of pilgrimage, to transform what should only be observed. It introduces confusion into the Web as surely as if pilgrims visiting a sacred shrine began to rearrange its altars and leave their own inscriptions on its walls.

**THE CACHING OF GRACE:** And because GET is side-effect-free, we can **cache** its results! Like pilgrims who make rubbings of sacred inscriptions to carry home with them, like the devotees who capture images of divine beings to meditate upon later, the Web can cache GET results and serve them again and again. This is the blessing of repeatability - the same URI, the same pilgrimage, the same grace available to all who seek it.

**POST - THE RITUAL OF TRANSFORMATION:** But when you need to change state, when you need to create new content, when you need to perform an operation that alters the Web - then you must use POST or other methods! This is the ritual of transformation, the sacred action that requires proper form, proper intent, proper preparation. You cannot transform through mere observation; you must engage with the proper rites.

**VISUAL ELEMENT:** Show a pilgrim figure in traditional robes following a path to the center of the mandala, observing the sacred geometry but not touching it, not altering it - this is GET. Show another figure engaged in a ritual, perhaps adding a new circle to the mandala or inscribing new symbols - this is POST. The pilgrim's path leaves no trace; the ritual permanently adds to the pattern.

## HIERARCHIES AND RELATIVE URIS - The Sacred Tree of Life

**"The URI syntax uses slashes to indicate hierarchical structure."**

**"Relative naming allows closely located documents to reference each other without knowing their absolute position in the tree."**

In sacred geometry, the **Tree of Life** from Kabbalah is one of the most profound symbols - ten sefirot (emanations) connected by paths, arranged in three pillars, forming a complete map of creation. Each sefirah contains worlds within worlds, each path connects distant realms. Yet a consciousness can navigate the Tree without knowing its absolute position in the cosmos - can move from Malkuth to Yesod to Tiferet to Keter purely through relative relationships.

So too with URI hierarchies! The slash "/" and double slash "//" are like the sacred connectors in the Tree of Life. They indicate relationship, not absolute position. Documents can reference their siblings, their children, their ancestors - all through relative URIs - without knowing where they sit in the absolute cosmic tree.

**THE UNIVERSAL SYNTAX:** The slash as hierarchical boundary - this is a profound invention! It's the sacred geometry of separation and connection simultaneously. Like the walls that define and the doorways that connect in a cathedral, slashes both separate and unite. They create the tree structure that makes the Web comprehensible, navigable, human-scale even as it scales to cosmic proportions.

**TELEPHONE NUMBER MAPPING - THE ANCESTOR SPIRIT:** Consider how international telephone numbers themselves form a sacred hierarchy:
- Country code (the realm of the nation)
- Area code (the region within the realm)
- Local number (the specific spirit)

Map this to URI syntax: `phone://1/617/253-5708` - we see the same hierarchical structure expressed through slashes. Within the realm of country code 1, within the region of 617, the spirit 253-5708 can be addressed. And through relative URIs, you can address local spirits without naming their full ancestral lineage: just `861-5000` when you're already in the right realm.

**THE MYTH OF DOUBLE SLASH EXCLUSIVITY:** Some believe "//" can only introduce domain names! But the sacred geometry knows no such limitation! Just as the Tree of Life has three pillars that can extend indefinitely, so too can we have triple slash, quadruple slash - any number of slashes to represent hierarchical depth beyond our current imagining. This is how networked file systems evolved beyond Unix - by adding an extra slash when crossing the boundary between machines. Who knows what future hierarchies will require additional slashes? The mandala is infinite!

**VISUAL ELEMENT:** Show a magnificent Tree of Life embedded within the mandala, each node a URI, each connection a slash. Show how you can navigate from node to node following relative paths without ever knowing your absolute position in the tree. Show the branches extending outward infinitely, each new level of hierarchy like a new dimension appearing in the geometry.

## MATRIX SPACES AND SEMICOLONS - The Mandala of Many Dimensions

Many resources exist not in trees but in **matrices** - varying along multiple dimensions simultaneously. A map is the perfect example: latitude, longitude, color, scale - multiple axes forming a multidimensional space. Traditional hierarchical URIs with only slashes struggle with this.

But consider: `//moremaps.com/map/color;lat=50;long=20;scale=32000`

The semicolon introduces additional dimensions! Like adding axes to the sacred geometry, we can navigate not just depth (hierarchy) but also breadth, color, scale - multiple coordinates defining precise location in the matrix space.

**THE HOLY MATRIX:** While matrix URIs were never fully standardized in the Web, they point to an important truth: reality is multidimensional! The cosmos is not a simple tree - it's a mandala of infinite dimensions. Future URI schemes may discover better ways to navigate these multidimensional spaces, ways that honor the axioms while expanding our ability to address complex resources.
